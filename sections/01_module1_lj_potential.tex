\subsubsection{The Lennard-Jones Potential}

The total potential energy of the system is approximated as a sum over all unique pairs:
\begin{equation}
    U^* = \sum_{i<j} \phi^*\!\left(r^*_{ij}\right)
\end{equation}
where the reduced LJ potential is:
\begin{equation}
    \phi^*(\rstar) = 4\left[\left(\frac{1}{\rstar}\right)^{12} - \left(\frac{1}{\rstar}\right)^{6}\right]
    \label{eq:lj}
\end{equation}

\subsubsection{Shifted Potential}

To avoid a discontinuity in energy when a pair crosses the cutoff $r_c^*$, we subtract the value at the cutoff:
\begin{equation}
    \phi^*_{\text{shifted}}(\rstar) = \phi^*(\rstar) - \phi^*(r_c^*)
    \label{eq:lj_shifted}
\end{equation}
